Resume

I've always loved math, science, and chemistry, which led me to pursue a degree in Chemical Engineering. Part-way through my degree, I had my first experience with computer science and immediately fell in love. Though I decided to stick with my chemE program, I continued to learn and love computer science throughout my bachelor's and beyond, finding ways to automate my work wherever I could. Finally, in 2019, I decided to pursue a degree in computer science, which brought me right back to Georgia Tech. I continue to be amazed by this incredible discipline. Although I've found interest in each of my courses, from high-performance computer architecture, to advanced operating systems, to human computer interaction, I've decided to pursue work in full-stack and back-end development. I’ve learned that abstracting my programs (by first defining rock-solid logic and then building sophisticated interfaces for it) plays well to my organizational strengths and is one of my favorite parts of development.
